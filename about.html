Embrace Learning at University of Ghana

The University of Ghana, the premier and largest university in Ghana, was founded as the University College of the Gold Coast by Ordinance on August 11, 1948, with the aim of providing and promoting university education, learning, and research. It stands as one of Africa's most eminent public universities. Our vision and mission are to become a “World Class research-intensive University” while creating an enabling environment that makes the University of Ghana increasingly relevant to national and global development through cutting-edge research, high-quality teaching, and learning. Our impact on individuals, our region, and the world is profound, whether launching young people into a boundless future or confronting the grand challenges of our time through dauntless research. UG educates more than 38,000 students annually, with approximately 2.64% pursuing computer science. We translate ideas into impactful change, transforming the world around us. For more about our impact, visit our news site, UG News."

Join Our Academic Community

In today's Information Technology era, Computer Science stands as its cornerstone. Understanding computer science is pivotal for society to fully leverage the advantages of Information Technology and make significant strides in economic progress. It encompasses the exploration and creation of applications that facilitate our daily use of computers, the supportive software systems, and the methodologies used to develop dependable, ethical applications and systems. Computer Science primarily focuses on the skill of computer programming. Employers seeking Computer Scientists anticipate proficient programmers equipped with up-to-date knowledge of techniques and tools, capable of applying robust engineering principles in crafting, constructing, and maintaining computer programs. The array of departmental courses and skills acquired by students open doors to diverse employment opportunities across various sectors of the economy. Our curriculum prepares graduates for the computing industry, fostering teamwork within project or research and development settings while equipping them with specialized skills. Additionally, the program serves as a launchpad for further studies or research, exposing students to established and emerging technologies, methodologies, and active research fields in Computer Science.
